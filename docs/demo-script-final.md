# Demo Script Final — My Address, My City

**Official demo script for Phase 4 submission.**  
Target duration: **60–90 seconds**

**Author:** Aicardo Barco Fajardo · abarcof@gmail.com

---

## What Is Implemented (Do Not Mention Otherwise)

- This Address: zoning, flood risk, neighborhood, council district, property record, trash schedule
- What's Closest: nearest park, community center, hospital
- Nearby City Records: code violations, building permits (last 12 months, 0.5 miles)
- City Resources: four official city links (report issue, permits, public records, stay informed) + Request alerts
- Civic Snapshot Summary: compact summary at top of panel
- Demo presets: City Hall, Residential, Outside Montgomery
- About This Data: method transparency drawer
- Copy Link: shareable deep link to snapshot

---

## Before the Demo

- App open in a browser tab, map visible
- No address selected — clean start
- Test the flow once privately before recording
- Backup: use demo preset "City Hall" if search fails

---

## Flow

### Opening (5 seconds)

> "This is My Address, My City — a civic web app for Montgomery, Alabama. You give it one address, and it tells you everything a resident needs to know."

---

### Step 1 — Search or Preset (5 seconds)

**Action:** Either type `103 N Perry St, Montgomery, AL` in the search bar, or click the **City Hall** demo preset.

> "Let's start with City Hall."

Map zooms, marker appears, Civic Snapshot Summary and panel populate.

---

### Step 2 — This Address (10 seconds)

**Action:** "This Address" tab is active by default.

> "We see zoning, flood zone, neighborhood, and council district — all from Montgomery city GIS, in plain language."

Point to the Civic Snapshot Summary at the top, then the cards. Do not read every field.

---

### Step 3 — What's Closest (10 seconds)

**Action:** Click the "What's Closest" tab.

> "Nearest park, community center, hospital — with approximate straight-line distance. The app labels these clearly so residents understand they're not driving distances."

Point to the map markers and distance labels.

---

### Step 4 — Nearby City Records (12 seconds)

**Action:** Click the "Nearby City Records" tab.

> "Code violations and building permits — within half a mile and from the last 12 months only. The app shows how many recent records and a few examples."

Point to the "Showing X recent" badges and cards.

> "This turns a static lookup into neighborhood awareness."

---

### Step 5 — City Resources (8 seconds)

**Action:** Click the "City Resources" tab.

> "The app connects residents to action — report an issue, apply for permits, request public records, or stay informed. All links go to official city resources."

Point to the links. Do not click them.

---

### Step 6 — Out of Bounds (Optional, 5 seconds)

**Action:** Click the **Outside Montgomery** demo preset.

> "And if someone selects a location outside Montgomery, we show a clear message — this app is for city addresses only."

---

### Closing (5 seconds)

> "One address. Your city. Clear answers. My Address, My City is built to scale — same pattern for any city with open data. That's the vision."

|---------|----------|
| Search fails | Use the **City Hall** demo preset |
| One tab fails | Skip to the next tab |
| App is slow | Have a screen recording ready |
| Data empty | "This location doesn't have that data — the app shows that clearly" |

---

## Principles

1. **Show, don't explain.** Let the UI speak.
2. **Never read field names aloud.** Summarize in plain language.
3. **Keep moving.** Never debug live.
4. **End strong.** End with the tagline and commercialization hint.
5. **Stay under 90 seconds.**
