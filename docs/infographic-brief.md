---

## The Problem

- Residents struggle to get a clear picture of their address
- Information scattered across GIS viewers, dashboards, department websites
- Most tools built for analysts, not residents
- No single place answers: "What's true about my address?"

---

## The Solution

A resident-facing civic web app. Enter one address or click the map → get a civic snapshot in plain language. One screen, all answers.

**Data source:** City of Montgomery official GIS (gis.montgomeryal.gov)

---

## Three Questions the App Answers

1. **What is true about this address?**  
   Zoning · Flood risk · Neighborhood · Council district · Property record · Trash schedule

2. **What is closest?**  
   Nearest park · Community center · Hospital (with approximate distance)

3. **What is happening nearby?**  
   Code violations · Building permits (last 12 months, within 0.5 miles)

---

## Four Modules (Tabs)

| Tab | Content |
|-----|---------|
| **This Address** | Zoning, flood risk, neighborhood, council district, property, trash |
| **What's Closest** | Nearest park, community center, hospital |
| **Nearby City Records** | Code violations, building permits |
| **City Resources** | Report issues, apply for permits, request records, stay informed |

---

## What Makes It Different

- **Address-first** — Start from the resident's address, not from data layers
- **Resident-facing** — Plain language, no GIS jargon
- **Neighborhood awareness** — Recent code violations and permits in one place
- **Single-screen UX** — No complex navigation
- **Bright Data integration** — Optional official web updates from city domains

---

## Commercialisation Potential

- **Product:** White-label civic information for mid-size US cities
- **Pilot:** Montgomery, Alabama
- **Scale:** ~300 mid-size cities with open data portals
- **Model:** City licensing or civic SaaS deployment
- **Customers:** City governments, civic tech teams, local organizations
